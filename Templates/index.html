Food adulteration

Food adulteration refers to the act of intentionally adding inferior, harmful, or cheaper substances to food products, compromising their quality and safety. It is a significant concern as it poses health risks to consumers and undermines trust in the food industry.

Causes and Effects

Economic gain, lack of regulatory oversight, unethical practices, and supply chain complexities. Profit-driven motives, substitution of ingredients, and improper handling can contribute to adulteration.

Food adulteration poses significant health risks to consumers, leading to foodborne illnesses, allergic reactions, and long-term health complications. It erodes consumer trust, compromises food quality and safety, and has detrimental impacts on public health and well-being.
